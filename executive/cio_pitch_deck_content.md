# ECS — CIO Pitch Deck Content (15 slides)

Each slide: **Headline · On-slide content · Speaker notes.** Numbers align with the ECS ROI
model and demo baseline. Designed for a 20–25 minute CIO/Board session.

---

## Slide 1 — Executive Summary
**Headline:** ECS — Collect Once. Reuse Everywhere.
**On-slide:** One platform that automates audit & compliance evidence, reuses it across every
framework, and gives the board live audit readiness. Live value today: **₹17.27 Cr**. Net
benefit at scale: **₹143.1 Cr** on stable **₹2.2 Cr** OPEX.
**Speaker notes:** "In one line: we turn audit from a recurring manual cost into an automated,
reusable asset — with leadership visibility we've never had."

## Slide 2 — The Business Problem
**Headline:** Audit & compliance is manual, repetitive, and invisible.
**On-slide:** Same audits every year (VAPT, PCI DSS, IS Audit, baselines) across hundreds of
apps · evidence chased over email · observations tracked in spreadsheets · no live board view.
**Speaker notes:** "The cost isn't one audit — it's the same effort repeated across every
framework, every cycle, with no reuse and no visibility."

## Slide 3 — Current State
**Headline:** Fragmented tools, duplicated effort.
**On-slide:** Evidence scattered across systems · framework teams working in silos · audit prep
is a fire-drill · leadership sees status only at quarter-end.
**Speaker notes:** "Every framework owner re-collects the same evidence. That duplication is the
opportunity."

## Slide 4 — ECS Vision
**Headline:** An evidence-reuse engine for the bank.
**On-slide:** Collect evidence once → map to many frameworks → automate workflows → surface
board-grade insight. Banking-native by design.
**Speaker notes:** "ECS is opinionated for banking — RBI, PCI, CSITE, baselines built in, not
configured over months."

## Slide 5 — Architecture
**Headline:** Enterprise-grade, container-native.
**On-slide:** FastAPI app · Postgres + pgvector (RAG) + Redis + MinIO · connectors
(ServiceNow/Jira/Git/SonarQube/Jenkins) · OIDC/JWT + RBAC + audit trail.
**Speaker notes:** "Modular, secure, and deployable in our environment — and it demos with zero
external dependencies."

## Slide 6 — Key Capabilities
**Headline:** Everything is live and drillable.
**On-slide:** Enterprise & Pan-India dashboards · framework analysis · reports (scheduled,
evidence-linked) · trends & forecasting · drill-anywhere to evidence/observation/owner.
**Speaker notes:** "No slideware. Every number on screen drills to the underlying evidence."

## Slide 7 — AI Capabilities
**Headline:** Ask in plain language.
**On-slide:** Grounded Copilot (RAG) · audit summarization · evidence Q&A with citations ·
AI-Ops assistant · AI-SDLC governance · roadmap to agentic remediation.
**Speaker notes:** "Leadership shouldn't need to know where to click. Ask, and get a cited
answer from our own evidence."

## Slide 8 — Executive Dashboards
**Headline:** The board sees readiness in one screen.
**On-slide:** Enterprise compliance **84.6%** · national score **82.2%** · 212 open observations
· Pan-India heatmap (North 87.4% → West 71.3%) · SLA on-time **91%**.
**Speaker notes:** "This is the board view — posture, geography, and trend, all live."

## Slide 9 — ROI
**Headline:** A clear, defensible return.
**On-slide:** Live value **₹17.27 Cr** · 25-app model **₹4.54 Cr** / **45.4K hours** / **22.7
FTE** · scale-up Year-7 net benefit **₹143.1 Cr** on **₹2.2 Cr** OPEX.
**Speaker notes:** "Conservative, expected and aggressive scenarios are modeled — even
conservative is compelling."

## Slide 10 — Risk Reduction
**Headline:** Fewer breaches, faster closure.
**On-slide:** SLA breaches 18 → 8 (Nov→Mar) · rejections 8.2% → 4.2% · 46 open risks tracked,
11 high/critical surfaced to the board.
**Speaker notes:** "We reduce both the operational risk of missed SLAs and the assurance risk of
being unprepared."

## Slide 11 — Compliance Benefits
**Headline:** Continuous, banking-native compliance.
**On-slide:** 20 frameworks · coverage 73% → 81% trending · collect-once → map-to-many ·
regulatory change feed (roadmap).
**Speaker notes:** "Coverage is rising while effort falls — that's the reuse effect."

## Slide 12 — Operational Efficiency
**Headline:** Automation replaces email chasing.
**On-slide:** 142 collection jobs/day · 96.1% connector health · evidence reuse 34.5% ·
scheduled audit-ready exports.
**Speaker notes:** "The teams get their time back; the platform does the chasing."

## Slide 13 — Product Roadmap
**Headline:** Harden → Automate → Intelligence → Platform → Scale → Leadership.
**On-slide:** R1 Executive-Ready Core · R2 Automation & Integration · R3 Intelligence ·
R4 Enterprise Platform · R5 Scale/Multi-tenancy · R6 Market Leadership.
**Speaker notes:** "A credible 3-year path, each release self-funding through incremental ROI."

## Slide 14 — Investment Ask
**Headline:** Fund R1–R2 to reach production at scale.
**On-slide:** Stable ₹2.2 Cr annual OPEX · investment in async collection, connectors, SSO,
observability, AI summarization · payback within Year 1 at expected scenario.
**Speaker notes:** "We're asking to harden and scale what already works — not to build from
scratch. Payback is fast."

## Slide 15 — Closing
**Headline:** ECS is ready to scale across the estate.
**On-slide:** Collect once. Reuse everywhere. Board-grade visibility. Predictable cost. ₹143.1
Cr net benefit at scale.
**Speaker notes:** "Approve R1–R2 and we move from a proven demo to enterprise rollout. I'll take
questions."
